enabLED

Crutch enhancement suite to improve the experience of using crutches.
UX, Product

See the full documentation here.

Project Intro

enabLED is a crutch enhancement suite that uses an accelerometer, a force sensitive resistor, LEDs, and a GPS to visualize and map crutch usage. enabLED is our response to the difficulty and burden of using crutches.
enabLED was originally created by Paige Xio Alvarez, Lee Hamstra, Brian Le, and myself for Eric Paulos' UC Berkeley course, Critical Making.

The Problem

After exploring the aspects of human-powered transportation, both its wonders as well as its faults, we decided to focus on mobility aids, specifically crutches. We know that the road to recovery is often arduous and frustrating, so we sought to make the process more fun and empowering.

Brainstorming with crutches in mind.

User Research

We interviewed friends and peers who were using crutches due to injury. We shared with them our initial ideas for functions and listened to struggles of their crutch-using experience.
Resounding opinions were frustration at the inability to find accessible routes and the feeling of being the minority as a crutch-user.

Initial Ideation

With making crutch-using more enjoyable and convenient in mind, we decided to gamify the experience by creating a network of crutch-users to crowdsource data in order to map out crutch-friendly routes for other crutch-users.

Conclusion and Thoughts for the Future

Feedback from our peers expressed praise for the idea of crowdsourcing data and desire to see the expansion upon this idea into, for example, a Yelp-esque community for crutch-users to leave reviews and comments on difficult (or easy) routes.
Crowdsourcing really depends on a large and active community, thus research and better understanding of a more diverse mobility aid-using user group is needed.
