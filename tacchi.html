Tacchi Studios Website

Redesigning Tacchi Studios' website.

Project Intro

Tacchi Studios is seeking to revamp their website in order to highlight their notable client work and attract future clients.
As an intern for Tacchi Studios, I worked alongside our UX product manager to brainstorm different layouts and draft wireframes of the new website.

The Problem

Currently, the studios’ website showcases work in a relatively unorganized format. Our newest intern expressed that she could not differentiate what was client work and what were in-house projects. Even our UX product manager said that he didn’t know clicking certain images would bring him to case study pages.

User Research

Viewers of the website include potential clients, job seekers, and general people interested in the studios.

Initial Ideation

Our UX product manager brainstormed initial ideas to showcase our team’s working culture and our design and work process. After discussion with our studios’ director, the direction changed to emphasize our notable client work.
Working together with our UX product manager, I drafted several layouts of the home page, playing with the order of content to best highlight past client work while simultaneously expressing our studios’ personality and work style.

Observations through Iteration

We struggled with iterations that were too repetitive: flashing our clients’ logos with links to case studies right under our studios’ catchy one-liner, followed by larger highlights of client work also linking to case studies. But on the other hand iterations that were too minimal failed to give the studios an identity.

Secondary Ideation

On the home page, we decided to give each case study its own large section as well as sections explaining the studios’ mission and design process with minimal text but eye-catching images and icons.

Conclusion and Thoughts for the Future

Clients will be most drawn to notable client work but will also want to see what differentiates Tacchi Studios from other design agencies. Job seekers interested in working for the studios will want to see an emphasis on the studios’ work culture.
