my.Flow

The smart tampon and companion app.
UI, UX, Mobile App, Product

See the full documentation here.
Receiver of the Maker Faire Editor's Choice Award.

Project Intro

my.Flow is a smart tampon that senses saturation of the tampon, relays that information via Bluetooth to a companion mobile application, and sends a push notification to notify the user that her tampon needs to be changed.
my.Flow was originally created for Eric Paulos' course Critical Making at UC Berkeley by a team consisting of Amanda Brief, Drake Myers, Madeeha Ghori, Tomas Vega, and myself. Since then, we have been seeking funding to fully develop my.Flow as a product.
I led the design and development of the companion app.

The Problem

The topic of the period is treated like taboo when it should really be openly discussed, especially so that young women can learn and not internalize shame about their their bodies.
My team and I set out to change the direction of the period conversation by developing a smart device and a companion app.

User Research

We conducted surveys on 400 women (through Amazon's Mechanical Turk and asking them to send in video clips), asking them what kind of information they wanted about their menstrual cycle that they couldn't already get.

Results from Amazon's Mechanial Turk survey.

Initial Ideation

For the development of the companion mobile app, I researched existing period tracking apps while considering the our survey results. Since my.Flow collects data real-time through the tampon, my.Flow’s companion app will eliminate requiring user input and will instead focus on providing clear, simple analyses of the information collected from the tampon device.

Considering users and necessary functions of app.

Observations through Iterations

As we considered the app’s design and usability direction more, I realized that narrowing our target users was necessary.
This realization urged the design to appeal to more than one user groups.

Interactions

I kept the design as uncluttered as possible to focus on the main features of the app, the count-down timer and graph.
I minimized functions and made signifiers large to increase intuitiveness.

Conclusion and Thoughts for the Future

We presented our final deliverable to our peers and received positive feedback on our innovativeness and overall design of the app.
In thoughts for the future of my.Flow, much more research is needed on a much more specific target user group to refine and define who is in need of a product like my.Flow.
